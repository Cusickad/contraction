Representations and Warranties

Representations and warranties are promises about the state of the world. They are information statements, in which the parties promise that certain facts are true. This way, you can hold the other party accountable, not for any promises they fail to uphold, but for saying things that aren't true. Although representations cannot be about future states or events (but warranties can), and an unture representation has stronger penalties than an untrue warranty, they typically get lumped together.

This is where you put the state in which your tenant is incorporated. It's helpful to include because it makes your tenant promise that they are who they say they are and are a legal business. Note that this only applies to a tenant that's a business; if you're renting to an individual, you would omit this provision.
