What do I need to know about contracts?

What is a contract?

A contract is a written agreement between two (or sometimes more) parties. A "party" in this case is a person, business, or organization that is agreeing to something with another person, business, or organization. A contract memorializes an agreement; in other words, a contract should be a written record of what you're agreeing to, so you should have discussed it beforehand.

Why should I write a contract?

In a perfect world, you'd never need a written contract. Everyone would remember and stick to their promises. However, this is an imperfect world. A contract can help you and the other party remember what you each agreed to do. More importantly, a contract can hold the parties to their agreements. A contract is a legally binding document, which means that a court can uphold the terms of the agreement. This means that you shouldn't agree to something you can't or don't want to fulfill, as you can be forced to uphold it. So, a well-written contract can mean that you can hold the other party to their promises without inadvertently making a promise they didn't mean to.

What is a makes a good contract?

To understand what makes a good contract, it's important to know that a contract involves hoping for the best but preparing for the worst. Ideally, everyone will uphold the terms of their bargain with no issue. If you're turning to the contract, something has already gone wrong. So, in writing the contract, you need to prepare for things going wrong, which means you'll want to write a contract that courts will interpret in your favor. Courts generally try to find and uphold what the parties intended to agree to. As such, a good contract makes it clear what the parties are agreeing on. Ambiguity is bad, because it means room to interpret what the parties mean. Your contract should be as clear as possible and state everything that is part of your deal.

What are the parts of a contract?

Preamble

The Preamble is the introduction to the contract. It's where the most basic information about the contract is laid out: what type of contract it is, who it's between, and when it takes effect. Although you can go into more detail about these elements, making for a longer Preamble, the basic elements are simple.

Background

The Background (also called the "recitals") is an optional part of a contract. It explains why both parties are entering into the deal. It exists because it can help set the tone for a contract and it can help show the intentions of the parties if there comes a dispute. That being said, it's not officially "part of the agreement", so you can largely put whatever you want or omit it entirely.
